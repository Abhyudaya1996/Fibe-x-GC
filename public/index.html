Conservative estimate. No new users to acquire. No new product to build. Just credit cards — placed at the exact moments in the Fibe journey where users already need them.

01 · Why Bother?

Fibe disburses 3 lakh loans every month. Every borrower is income-verified, KYC-complete, and credit-scored.

That makes them exactly the customer banks want to give a credit card to. After their loan, the natural next step in their financial life is a credit card — for rewards, for a credit buffer, for EMI flexibility on the next purchase.

Right now that conversation happens somewhere else. On Paisabazaar, BankBazaar, or a bank's own website — with someone else capturing the commission. Fibe does the hard work of acquiring and qualifying the user, then hands them off for free.

02 · Why Great.Cards?

The alternative is building it yourself. That's a longer road than it looks.

Direct bank relationships take 12–18 months of legal, commercial, and compliance negotiation — per bank. Paisabazaar spent years building those. Building in-house means owning the card catalog, eligibility logic, bank APIs, and a compliance layer that needs permanent maintenance. That is a team and a budget that never goes away.

Great.Cards gives Fibe 100+ cards, a live eligibility engine, and an AI calling team through a single API. One sprint to embed. We handle the banks, the compliance, the calling, and the reporting.

03 · What Fibe Brings

The hard part is already done.

User acquisition, trust, KYC, income verification, credit scoring — Fibe has done all of it. By the time a user sees a Great.Cards recommendation, they are already pre-qualified. That is why conversion benchmarks on Fibe placements are structurally higher than cold-start aggregator flows.

Fibe brings the users and the trust. Great.Cards brings the product and the infrastructure. The revenue split reflects exactly that.
